Understanding Heart Health: A Comprehensive Guide for Modern Living

Heart disease remains the number one cause of death globally, yet most cardiac events are preventable with the right knowledge and early action. In this guide, Dr. Anita Sharma breaks down everything you need to know.

Why Heart Health Matters More Than Ever

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) accounts for approximately 17.9 million deaths per year globally. In India, the burden of heart disease is growing at an alarming rate — with the average age of a first heart attack dropping to 50 years, compared to 65+ in Western nations. Urbanisation, stress, sedentary lifestyles, and diets high in processed foods are significant contributors.

The good news? Up to 80% of premature heart disease and strokes are preventable through lifestyle modification and regular screening. That's why understanding your heart health risks — and acting on them — is one of the most important investments you can make.

Key Facts About Heart Disease
  • Heart attacks occur every 40 seconds in India.
  • High blood pressure is the #1 risk factor and often has no symptoms.
  • Diabetes doubles the risk of heart disease in women.
  • Smoking accounts for one-third of all coronary heart disease deaths.

The Major Risk Factors You Can Control

Not all risk factors are within our control — age, family history, and genetics play a role. However, the majority of risks are modifiable:

High Blood Pressure

Target: below 120/80 mmHg. Check it every 6 months.

High Cholesterol

LDL should be below 100 mg/dL. Fasting lipid profile annually.

Obesity

BMI above 25 significantly raises cardiac risk. Even 5-10% weight loss helps.

Smoking

Quitting smoking halves your cardiac risk within 1 year.

Warning Signs — When to Seek Immediate Care

Many people experiencing a heart attack delay calling for help. Don't wait. Seek emergency care immediately if you experience:

  • Chest pain, pressure, squeezing or tightness
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw, neck or back
  • Shortness of breath, especially with activity
  • Sudden dizziness, cold sweat, or nausea without clear cause
